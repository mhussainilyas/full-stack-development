Here is a time line of his life

Jinnah is revered as the founder of Pakistan and is often referred to as "Quaid-e-Azam," meaning "Great Leader." His vision for Pakistan was of a democratic state where all citizens, regardless of religion, would have equal rights. Here's a brief timeline highlighting key events in the life of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah:

  • 1876: Born on December 25 in Karachi, which was then part of British India.
  • 1892: At the age of 16, Jinnah went to London to study law at Lincoln's Inn.
  • 1896: Became one of the youngest Indians to be called to the Bar.
  • 1906: Joined the All India National Congress, advocating for Hindu-Muslim unity and self-governance.
  • 1913: Joined the All-India Muslim League, aiming to represent Muslim interests in India.
  • 1916: Played a significant role in the Lucknow Pact, which aimed to unite Hindus and Muslims against British rule.
  • 1920: Shifted focus from Hindu-Muslim unity to the distinct political rights of Muslims, advocating for separate electorates.
  • 1930: Delivered a famous speech at the All-India Muslim League session in Lahore, emphasizing the need for a separate Muslim identity.
  • 1940: The Lahore Resolution was passed on March 23, demanding independence for Muslims in northwestern India.
  • 1947: Played a crucial role in the negotiations for the partition of India. Pakistan was created on August 14, and Jinnah became the country's first Governor-General.
  • 1948: Jinnah passed away on September 11 in Karachi, just a year after the creation of Pakistan.
